W209 Project: Change.org Twitter Analysis Tool

Visualizations Background Team

Project Background

This project was designed to provide a demo of visualizations that could be used to quickly see the engagement levels on a change.org petition. The project was completed for the class W209, Data Visualization, from the Master in Information and Data Science (MIDS) program at University of California at Berkeley (UC Berkeley). This project specifically made visualizations that enable analytics of social media campaigns in India, through working directly with Change.org program staff on their Gates Foundation funded Women & Girls project in India.



Petition Background

The petition choosen for this project is one asking for "Justice for Kathua Rape Victim: 8yr old girl raped and murdered in Kathua, J&K" The introduction verbiage from change.org: "This petition is an initiative by a group of women from Jammu and Kashmir (names below) appealing for justice in the case of rape and murder of 8 year old girl in Hiranagar, Kathua."

If you are interested in reading more about the petition, it can be found here: Change.org petition link



change.org Background

Change.org is online petition website that allows user to make a keep track of petition designed to influence companies, political entities and other organizations to make policy changes. From the change.org website: "On Change.org, people everywhere are starting campaigns, mobilizing supporters, and working with decision makers to drive solutions."

Change.org seeks to change the narrative of women & girls issues in India from victimhood to being powerful initiators of change. At the same time, Change.org seeks to build their our own internal capacity to collect social media data more systematically so they can document one of the ways their work impacts social narratives. Our visualization will encode Twitter data to provide the activists the insight they need to make decisions in their campaign. Based on data from keyword filtering, the visualization will show public awareness of campaign issues such as women's safety, access to health care, discrimination in the workplace, and disabled access.

For more information visit change.org's website at: change.org link
